Abstract

        For decades researchers have been debating numerous theories as to what dark matter could be made up of.
        Dark matter makes up about 27% of our universe, yet the only thing we know about dark matter is that it
        has a gravitational effect on normal matter. 
        One such theory is that primordial black holes are what make up dark matter. The basic outline of the theory
        is that in the first few microseconds of the universe everything was hot and dense enough to form an unimaginable
        number of black holes and now these countless tiny black holes are littered across our universe and causing
        the gravitational effects we have come to call dark matter.
        This theory does not go unchallenged however, there are arguments against primordial black holes making up
        dark matter and, in this paper, I intend to take a closer look at the arguments for and against primordial
        black holes as dark matter and propose my own conclusion as to whether I believe that primordial black holes
        are a valid contender as a dark matter candidate.
